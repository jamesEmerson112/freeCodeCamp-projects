Sir Ken Robinson

"I believe our only hope for the future is to adopt a new conception of human ecology,
one in which we start to reconstitute our conception of the richness of human capacity."

Here's a time line of Sir Ken Robinson's life

  • 1950 - Born in Liverpool, England
  • 1972 - Finished Bachelor of Education drama at Bretton Hall College of Education
  • 1981 - Completed PhD at the University of London, researching drama and theatre in education
  • 1985 - Director of the Arts in Schools Project throughout England and Wales.
  • 1998 - Led a UK commission on creativity, education and the economy
  • 2003 - Was Made a Knight Bachelor for his services to the arts.
  • 2005 - Was Named as one of Time/Fortune/CNN's "Principal Voices'
  • 2006 - Presented "Do Schools Kill creativity?", the most watched TED talk of all time
  • 2010 - The Royal Society of Arts animated one of Robinson's speeches about changing education paradigms
  • 2013 - Presented "How to escape education's death valley" outlining the three principles crucial for the human mind to flourish
  • 2017 - The animation of one of his speeches has been viewed more than 15 million times
  • 2020 - Passed awaw at the age of 70
